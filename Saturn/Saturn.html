Saturn

Saturn's beautiful rings are relatively young. They may have formed in the era of the dinosaurs here on Earth.

Saturn is the sixth planet from the Sun and the second largest planet in our solar system. Adorned with a dazzling system of icy rings, Saturn is unique among the planets. It is not the only planet to have rings, but none are as spectacular or as complex as Saturn's. Like fellow gas giant Jupiter, Saturn is a massive ball made mostly of hydrogen and helium. The farthest planet from Earth discovered by the unaided human eye, Saturn has been known since ancient times. The planet is named for the Roman god of agriculture and wealth, who was also the father of Jupiter.

5 Need-to-Know Things About Saturn

1

Saturn is a gas giant like Jupiter. It is made mostly of hydrogen and helium.

 

2

Saturn has a thick atmosphere.

 

3

Saturn has a lovely set of seven main rings with spaces between them.

As of June 8, 2023, Saturn has 146 moons.

4

Four robotic spacecraft have visited Saturn, including Pioneer 11, Cassini, and Voyager 1 and 2.

5

When Galileo Galilei saw Saturn through a telescope in the 1600s, he wasn't sure what he was seeing. At first he thought he was looking at three planets, or a planet with handles. Now we know those "handles" turned out to be the rings of Saturn.
