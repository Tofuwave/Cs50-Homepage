Mars

The only planet we know of inhabited entirely by robots.

Mars is no place for the faint-hearted. It’s dry, rocky, and bitter cold. The fourth planet from the Sun, Mars is one of Earth's two closest planetary neighbors (Venus is the other). Mars is one of the easiest planets to spot in the night sky – it looks like a bright red point of light. Despite being inhospitable to humans, robotic explorers – like NASA's Perseverance rover – are serving as pathfinders to eventually get humans to the surface of the Red Planet.

7 Need-to-Know Things About Mars

1

Mars is a terrestrial planet. It is small and rocky.

 

2

Mars has a thin atmosphere.

 

3

Mars has an active atmosphere, but the surface of the planet is not active. Its volcanoes are dead.

Mars has two moons. Their names are Phobos and Deimos.

4

One day on Mars lasts 24.6 hours. It is just a little longer than a day on Earth.

5

Venus does not have any moons.

6

Several missions have visited Mars. And Mars is the only planet we have sent rovers to. They drive around Mars, taking pictures and measurements.

7

One year on Mars is 687 Earth days. It is almost twice as long as one year on Earth.
